Where it breaks

The failure pattern is familiar.

Most teams feel it long before they can explain it: the finance function is working harder, but confidence is not increasing.

Close never really closes

Wallet evidence, exchange data, and manual exceptions keep reopening the same reporting period.

Token policy lags reality

Issuance, incentives, staking, treasury actions, and impairment are operating faster than accounting conclusions.

Controls are implied, not designed

Access, approvals, key actions, reserves, and exceptions exist in practice but not in a credible control model.

Reserve reporting feels narrative-led

Stakeholders can see balances, but not the evidentiary path that makes those balances trustworthy.

Audit prep starts too late

Teams try to compress months of missing structure into the weeks right before fieldwork.

Board trust depends on translation

The company has activity, but no clean line from operational reality to a defensible financial narrative.
