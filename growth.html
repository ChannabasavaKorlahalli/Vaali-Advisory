What teams get wrong

The market rarely punishes lack of effort. It punishes misread dynamics.

Most growth plans fail because they assume alignment where none exists and call noise a funnel.

Attention gets mistaken for demand

Teams see a spike in activity and assume the system has found product-market fit or channel fit.

Incentives pull the wrong behavior

What looks like traction often reflects short-lived reward seeking rather than durable adoption.

Partnerships outrun internal ownership

Announcements happen before operating commitments, accountabilities, and revenue pathways are real.

BD narratives ignore power

Teams pursue counterparties without mapping who controls distribution, budget, compliance, or product leverage.

Launch plans assume coordination

Core team, ecosystem actors, channels, and community are treated as aligned when their incentives diverge sharply.

Revenue becomes episodic

Without a clear system, every deal is bespoke, every win is fragile, and compounding never starts.
